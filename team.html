Founding attorney

Deborah Stewart, Esq.

Attorney Deborah Stewart has been practicing law since 2010, primarily in business law, estate planning and probate. While she has successfully litigated both business and probate matters, she focuses most of her time on transactional work designed to keep clients out of the court system altogether.

She is a former banker and financial services consultant with one of the top accounting firms in the country. As a second-career attorney, Deborah thoroughly enjoys helping clients navigate the nuances of the legal system — especially when their concerns sit at the intersection of money, family, and the future.

Deborah is a graduate of Old Dominion University, where she received both her BS in Business Administration and her Master of Public Administration (MPA). After establishing her career, she fulfilled a lifelong dream of becoming an attorney, graduating from Atlanta's John Marshall Law School.

As a member of the Georgia Bar, Deborah is licensed to practice before all Georgia Superior Courts and the Georgia Supreme Court. She serves as court-appointed counsel for probate matters within Douglas County Probate Court, and represents clients in the Probate Courts of Fulton, DeKalb, Cobb, Gwinnett, and Rockdale Counties.

Outside the office, she enjoys spending time with her family, traveling, and serving in the community.
