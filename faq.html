Yes. A will isn't about wealth — it's about clarity. Without one, Georgia decides who inherits, who raises minor children, and how disputes are resolved. Even a modest estate (a home, retirement accounts, life insurance) benefits from a will plus a power of attorney and advance directive.

A will is a set of instructions that goes through probate court after you pass away. A revocable living trust holds your assets during your lifetime and transfers them privately at death, bypassing probate. We help you decide which is right based on your assets, family situation, and goals.
