Quick answers

Common questions, honestly answered.

Yes. A will is not about wealth — it's about clarity. Without one, Georgia decides who inherits, who raises your minor children, and how disputes are resolved. Even a modest estate (a home, retirement accounts, life insurance) benefits from a will plus a power of attorney and advance directive.

A will is a set of instructions that goes through probate court after you pass. A revocable living trust holds your assets during your lifetime and transfers them privately at death — bypassing probate. We help you decide which is right based on your assets, family situation, and goals.

Yesterday. Georgia Medicaid uses a 5-year "look-back" period, so the earlier we plan, the more options you have to protect family assets while qualifying for long-term care benefits. If you're already inside the look-back window, there are still strategies — come talk to us before making transfers.
