Probate

Probate in Georgia can feel overwhelming. It doesn't have to. We handle the filings, deadlines, and court appearances so your family can focus on grieving and remembering.

When a loved one passes or becomes unable to make their own decisions, families face a complicated court process at the worst possible moment. We step in to manage that process — with the experience and steadiness families need at a time of grief.

How we help you

  • Simple Estate Administration
  • Year's Support Petitions
  • Petition Filing
  • Letters Testamentary
  • Letters of Administration
  • Executor's Deeds
  • Adult or Child Guardianships (uncontested)
  • Adult or Child Conservatorships (uncontested)

Where we represent clients

Attorney Stewart represents clients in the Probate Courts of Fulton, DeKalb, Cobb, Gwinnett, and Rockdale Counties, and serves as court-appointed counsel for probate matters within Douglas County Probate Court. That means we know the local rules, the judges' preferences, and the practical timelines — which translates to fewer surprises for you.

Years' Support — a Georgia-specific tool

Georgia is one of the few states with a "Year's Support" procedure that can transfer a deceased spouse or minor child's property without a full estate administration. It's a powerful tool when used correctly — and it has strict deadlines. If you've recently lost a spouse and want to avoid full probate, ask us whether Year's Support applies to your situation.
