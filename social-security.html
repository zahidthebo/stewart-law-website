Social Security

If your application for Social Security benefits has been denied, the fight is not over. Most first applications are denied. That's where we come in.

The Social Security Administration process is confusing, slow, and statistically stacked against first-time applicants. The majority of people who apply for Social Security Disability are denied initially. That's exactly why securing experienced help matters.

If we take your case, we build a strategy designed to win — so you can begin to have the quality of life you deserve, regardless of the disability you're dealing with.

How we help you

  • Request for Reconsideration
  • Disability Hearing (ALJ)
  • Appeals Council Review
  • Medical Evidence & Witness Coordination
  • Vocational Expert Preparation
  • Long-Term Disability Strategy

The appeals process — in plain English

1. Reconsideration

Your first denial isn't the final word. We file a Request for Reconsideration, gather the medical evidence missing from your file, and address the specific reasons SSA gave.

2. Hearing before an Administrative Law Judge

If reconsideration fails, we go to a hearing. We prepare your testimony, brief your medical providers, and cross-examine vocational experts — the part of the process where having a lawyer matters most.

3. Appeals Council

If the ALJ rules against you, we can request review by the Social Security Appeals Council, then escalate to federal court if necessary.

The Stewart Law approach

We don't take every Social Security case. We evaluate honestly — and if we think you have a strong claim, we explain the path forward, the timeline, and the fee structure (which is regulated by federal law). No surprises.
